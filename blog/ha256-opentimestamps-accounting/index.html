How ECBTAX Verifies Credentials with SHA-256 and OpenTimestamps

Trust is becoming one of the most valuable business assets. In the digital economy, claims are easy to publish. Evidence is much harder to fake.

ECBTAX uses cryptographic verification methods to provide independent proof of authenticity and existence for professional credentials.

What is SHA-256?

SHA-256 is a cryptographic hashing algorithm.

It converts a document into a unique fingerprint. Even changing a single character creates a completely different hash.

For example:

28ec5257098242ac7e9c1430453fd228146430f2612e5fd177369561be9eaaab

This fingerprint uniquely identifies a document.

What is OpenTimestamps?

OpenTimestamps is an open-source protocol that proves a file existed before a certain moment in time.

Instead of trusting a central authority, the proof is anchored into the Bitcoin blockchain.

Why Use Bitcoin?

Bitcoin provides one of the most secure and decentralized public ledgers in the world.

Once a proof is anchored into a Bitcoin block, it becomes extremely difficult to alter or falsify.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can SHA-256 prove authenticity?

It proves integrity. If the file changes, the hash changes.

What does OpenTimestamps prove?

It proves that a file existed before a specific moment in time.

Why use Bitcoin?

Because Bitcoin provides a decentralized and highly secure public ledger.

Can anyone verify the proofs?

Yes. OpenTimestamps verification is public and independently reproducible.
