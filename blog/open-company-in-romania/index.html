How to Open a Company in Romania

Romania is one of the most attractive business destinations in Eastern Europe due to its EU membership, skilled workforce and competitive operating costs.

For foreign investors and entrepreneurs, opening a company in Romania is relatively straightforward, but it requires compliance with legal, accounting and tax requirements.

Why Romania?

  • European Union member state;
  • Strategic location;
  • Competitive labor costs;
  • Growing technology sector;
  • Access to international markets.

Step 1 – Choose the Business Structure

The most common legal form is the Romanian SRL (Limited Liability Company).

The choice depends on:

  • number of shareholders;
  • planned activity;
  • investment structure;
  • future growth plans.

Step 2 – Register the Company

The company must be registered with the Romanian Trade Registry.

Typical requirements include:

  • company name reservation;
  • articles of association;
  • registered office;
  • shareholder information;
  • activity codes.

Step 3 – Set Up Accounting

Romanian companies must maintain accounting records and comply with local reporting obligations.

Accounting should be organized immediately after incorporation.

Step 4 – VAT Analysis

Depending on the business model, VAT registration may be required or recommended.

Step 5 – Payroll and Employees

Companies hiring employees in Romania must comply with payroll, labor law and tax obligations.

Common Mistakes

  • delaying accounting setup;
  • ignoring VAT implications;
  • lack of tax planning;
  • insufficient reporting processes;
  • poor documentation.
