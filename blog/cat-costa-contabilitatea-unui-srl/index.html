Accounting Services in Romania for Foreign Investors

English guide for foreign investors needing accounting, payroll and tax advisory in Romania.

Summary: this article explains the issue, where it appears in business operations, how to handle it correctly and how to verify a professional provider.

Why it matters

Accounting, payroll and tax advisory are not just administrative tasks. They affect cash flow, tax risk, reporting quality, management decisions and compliance in Romania.

Where the issue appears

The issue appears whenever a company invoices clients, receives supplier documents, hires employees, registers for VAT, reports taxes or interacts with Romanian authorities.

  • invoices and accounting documents;
  • bank statements and contracts;
  • payroll and employee records;
  • VAT and tax filings;
  • ANAF communication and controls.

How it works

A professional provider starts by understanding the company's business model, not by mechanically processing documents. The process should include document mapping, fiscal calendar, recurring checks and management reporting.

How to verify

Common mistakes

  • choosing only by price;
  • not checking professional credentials;
  • unclear scope of work;
  • poor communication;
  • no preventive tax review.
